History of Pope John Paul II

  • 1920 Pope John Paul II was born in Poland on the 18th of May 1920, to the family of Carol Wojtyla and Emilia Kaczorowska. He was the youngest of 3 children. John Paul was baptised a month after his birth and had his first holy communion at the age of 9. He got confirmed at the age of 18.
  • 1946 He was ordained a Catholic priest on 1st November 1946(All Saints Day).
  • 1958 He was made an auxillary Bishop by Pope Pius XXI on 4th July 1958.
  • 1978 John Paul became head of the Catholic church and Soverign of the vatican city on 16th October 1978 after a papal conclave. Pope John Paul, who was a cardinal then, was elected as the Pope on the third day of conclave, in 1978, and adopted the name of his predecessor(Pope Paul IV), as atribute to him. Pope Paul was the 2nd Non-Italian Pope, after Pope Andrian IV(was pope in 16th century).
  • 1978 He was the longest serving Pope in modern history(1978-2005).
  • 2009 Pope Benedict XVI, his successor,proclaimed Pope John Paul venerable(Life of Virtue) on 19th December, 2009.
  • 2011 Also, Pope John Paul was beatified(a deceased person's entrance into heaven) on May 1st 2011. The date is also known/reffered to in the Catholic church as Divine Mercy Sunday. It is the Catholic church's tradition to celebrate Saint's feast days on the annivasary of their deaths, but that of Pope John Paul II he is celebrated on the annivasary of his Papal inauguration, being 22nd october.
  • 2005 On 1st February 2005, John Paul was hospitalised with breathing issue. He died on 2nd of April in 2005 at 15:30pm, This were his final words: "Allow me depart to the house of the father". He was buried on 8th of April 2005. His funeral was attended by leaders accross the world.
